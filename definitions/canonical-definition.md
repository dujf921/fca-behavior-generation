# Canonical Definition | Human Behavior FCA

Human Behavior FCA (Functional Causal Architecture) is a functional causal architecture for explaining how human behavior is generated, stabilized, interrupted, locked in, and updated through reality-facing feedback.

Its analysis object is the behavior generation chain, not personality type, not psychological labeling, not motivation theory, and not a chatbot wrapper.

Observable output is the entry point for analysis, not the final causal conclusion.

The purpose of Human Behavior FCA is to locate functional breakpoints in generation, maintenance, and update loops, rather than assigning human labels.
